How Turlock cops 'conspired to conceal Korey Kauffman's murder'

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Tuesday, September 10, 2024

Three California Highway Patrol officers who were arrested on Friday were linked to the murder of a car thief after allegedly helping with the cover up, alongside a prominent criminal defense attorney and his family and two liquor store owners.

Officers Walter Wells, Scott McFarlane and Eduardo Quintanar Jr are all accused of having a hand in the elaborate cover up, as all three shared a friendship with the suspects.

In the case that has rocked the city of Turlock, defense attorney Frank Carson, 60, was charged with the first-degree murder of 26-year-old Korey Kauffman, who disappeared from his hometown of Modesto three years ago.

Kauffman, a known scrap metal thief, was allegedly shot dead while trying to steal an antique car from Carson's house in March 2012, according to a police affidavit obtained by DailyMail.com.

Carson, his wife, Georgia Defilippo, 63, daughter, Christina Defilippo, 36, Wells, McFarlane Quintanar and two liquor store owners were arrested in Turlock on Friday.

A ninth suspect, Robert Lee Woody, was arrested and charged with murder in the case last year.

The tangled web surrounding the death of Korey Kauffman is reminiscent of HBO's True Detective, set in the fictitious Californian town of Vinci, where small-town politicians and corrupt police conspire to cover-up the death of a local businessman. 

The three highway patrol officers appeared to have close ties with the liquor store owners, Baljit Athwal and Daljit Atwal, who each face murder charges.

Wells, an officer with California Highway Patrol’s Merced office at the time of the investigation, had invested $10,000 into the brothers’ business.

After Kauffman was thought to have been murdered on March 30, 2012, his cellphone pinged to a tower that covers Wells’ residence. Wells’ phone pinged to the same tower. Police believe Wells was in possession of both phones at the time.

McFarlane, an officer with the California Highway Patrol’s Merced office, was Kauffman’s next door neighbor.

In intercepted phone calls, he is heard telling Atwal and Athwal not to speak to authorities or private investigators.

He also told Athwal, Atwal, Woody and Quintanar that Kauffman was a thief who ‘had to go’, according to the 325-page Stanislaus County Sheriff's Department affidavit.

Quintanar, an officer with the California Highway Patrol Office’s Merced and Modesto offices, also told Atwal and Athwal not to speak to investigators, claims the affidavit.

He also told the brothers tips on how to avoid tracking devices and how to see if someone had put one on their vehicle.

Atwal and Athwal had befriended the officers by throwing them parties in order to be forewarned if problems arose with their store.

And following Kauffman’s killing, the three California Highway Patrol officers apparently obstructed the investigation by giving false information due to their friendships with the suspects, it is reported.

His body was then secretly removed from the property and dumped in rural Mariposa County near Yosemite National Park. It was discovered by hunters in August 2013, sparking a murder inquiry.

Carson, who entered the race for Stanislaus County District Attorney last November, allegedly orchestrated Kauffman's killing due to his belief that the man was stealing valuable antiques from his home. The thief was taking these from storage containers on his property, Carson thought.

The lawyer had grown angry with people repeatedly stealing items from his Turlock house - and wanted revenge, according to the affidavit.

The attorney was 'actively trying to catch anybody who came on to his property' at the time that Kauffman was killed - and had reportedly even previously threatened the thief's friend with death.

He had told Michael Cooley, whom he also believed was stealing from him, 'If I f***ing catch you... or any of your f***ing friends over on my property I will kill you,' a neighbor claims in the affidavit.

Cooley was with Kauffman, a neighbor of Carson, on the night of March 29, 2012, when the pair reportedly discussed sneaking onto Carson's property to steal irrigation equipment from him.

According to Woody's son, Robert Woody Jr, the two men also planned to steal an antique car.

The duo jumped over Carson's fence, but were unaware that Woody and the two store owners charged in the case - Athwal and Atwal - were on the property, Woody Jr told police.

Crime scene: Kauffman's body was reportedly secretly removed from Carson's property (seen on Friday) and dumped in rural Mariposa County near Yosemite National Park. It was found by hunters in August 2013

When they realized, Woody and a third would-be thief - who has not been identified - then backed out and leaped back over the fence, it is said. However, Kauffman apparently did not do so.

Woody Jr said his father had told him he then heard gun shots ring out from the residence - where Carson is also believed to have been present - and that these resulted in the death of Kauffman.

Woody later told his son that the thief's body had been removed from the house and taken to 'the mountains near Yosemite National Park', where it was found 17 months later, the affidavit states.

Until this week, Woody was the only person charged in relation to the alleged murder and cover-up.

But now, Carson is facing a first-degree murder charge, along with counts of criminal conspiracy, false imprisonment and two counts of soliciting a crime. He denies any involvement in the killing.

Meanwhile, Mrs Defilippo has been charged with first-degree murder, criminal conspiracy and false imprisonment in the case following her arrest on Friday, according to the Modesto Bee.

And the lawyer's daughter is being held on charges of criminal conspiracy and being an accessory.

California Highway Patrolman Walter Wells has also been charged with murder, as well as criminal conspiracy and false imprisonment. 

Quintanar and McFarlane have been charged with criminal conspiracy and accessory.  

Brothers Athwal and Atwal - who own the Pop-N-Cork Liquor store in Turlock and spell their surnames differently - are also facing murder raps in relation to the disturbing case.

Cooley had a long-running dispute with Carson prior to Kauffman's killing. Even police have had to deal with their past confrontations, according to reports seen by the Turlock Journal.

Allegations: According to Robert Woody Jr - the son of suspect, Robert Woody, who was charged in the case last year - Kauffman was trying to steal an antique car from Carson's home on the night he was murdered

Gun shots: Woody Jr said his father had told him he heard gun shots at the residence. Woody later told his son that the thief's body had been removed and transported to 'the mountains near Yosemite National Park'

Kauffman, meanwhile, made his living selling scrap metal, that was sometimes stolen.

This week, his father Tony said he did not approve of his son's lifestyle, but insisted he did not deserve to die. He told the Modesto Bee: 'The whole scrapper life, I didn't approve of it.

'You know how fathers are with their sons. We tend to be harder on them. But he chose his own path. He was a good kid with a good heart and he didn't deserve what he got.'

Carson's attorney, Percy Martinez, says his client is innocent and his arrest was politically motivated.

Carson ran unsuccessfully for district attorney last year. He has been under investigation since Kauffman disappeared, Martinez said. Investigators searched Carson's property in 2012.

'The entire department and I are appalled at the mere thought that one former and two current employees played any role in this incident,' CHP Commissioner Joe Farrow said.

According to the affidavit, Carson has previously been accused of misconduct at the courthouse and in jail, as well as showing a 'defiance of law' by lying to officers.

On one occasion, the lawyer - said to be 'like a brother' to store owner Atwal - apparently grabbed a prisoner by the collar and shook him, so his feet were off the ground and his body was 'rigid'.

He is also accused of taking the alleged thefts on his property into his own hands. He previously said that he wanted Cooley 'dealt with to send a message not to f*** with my shit,' it is reported.

And he referred to Cooley and Kauffman together as the 'white trash twins', it is said.

Carson had motion detectors at his home that alerted him to thieves.

Wells - the cop charged with murder in the case - left the CHP several weeks ago.

The other two officers have been placed on administrative leave, Farrow said.

All of the suspects remained jailed on Saturday.
