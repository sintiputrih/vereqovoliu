Belinda Peregrín is a famous Spanish-Mexican singer who is also a songwriter and an actress. She started her career with acting at a very young age and then moved on to music. In 2003, she released her self-titled debut album, which has sold more than two and a half million copies. She has also played the lead role of an actress in soap operas like Camaleones.

    Belinda Peregrín Facts

  • In 2000, at the age of 10, she played the lead role in the Mexican children’s show, Amigos x siempre.
  • She appeared in a Mexican telenovela titled Aventuras en el tiempo and played the role of Violeta in 2001 and in 2002 she gained recognition for playing twins in Cómplices Al Rescate.
  • In 2007, she was nominated for 2 Latin Grammys, which included “Song of the Year” for Bella Traicion and “Best Female Pop Vocal Album” for Utopia.
  • She has appeared in motion pictures such as the Disney Channel original movie, The Cheetah Girls 2 (2006) and has dubbed the voice roles for The Tale of Despereaux (2008) and Las aventuras de Tadeo Jones (2012).
  • Peregrín worked with Zac Efron and Dwayne Johnson in the 2017 film, Baywatch.
