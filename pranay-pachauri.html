    Pranay Pachauri Facts

  • He made his first film appearance as Mihir (Gaurav’s older brother who is gay) in the family drama film Time Out in 2015. He co-starred in the film alongside Chirag Malhotra.
  • From 2015 to 2016, Pranay Pachauri appeared as Nishant in the comedy crime drama show Alisha.
  • He was cast in the recurring role of Sachin Ghatge in the 2018 Hindi comedy web series PM Selfiewallie. The show was created by Ekta Kapoor.
  • Pranay Pachauri played the recurring role of Zubin (Mukti’s fiancé and later husband) in the romantic comedy-drama television series Kaisi Yeh Yaariaan in 2018.
  • In 2018, he was seen as Shiv in the adventure comedy series The Trip. It stars Shweta Tripathi, Sapna Pabbi, and Mallika Dua.
  • He was cast as Damodar Kondapalli in the comedy miniseries Tathaastu in 2019.
  • He portrayed the character of Rifleman Yash Paul Sharma in the Hindi-language biographical war film Shershaah in 2021. Based on the life of Vikram Batra who was killed in action in the Kargil War, the film stars Sidharth Malhotra and Kiara Advani.
  • Pranay Pachauri brought to life the character of Nikhil Bajpai in the Hindi-language family-drama web television series Salt City in 2022. The show stars Piyush Mishra, Divyenndu, and Gauahar Khan.
  • Alongside the likes of Ashutosh Rana, Anjana Sukhani, Chandan Roy Sanyal, Paoli Dam, Rajesh Khattar, Rituraj Singh, and Satish Kaushik, Pranay Pachauri co-starred as Samar Shastri in the Hindi-language crime drama web series Karm Yuddh in 2022.
  • He played Shubhrat Khanna (Shubhangi’s cousin and L. N. Khanna’s grandson) in the Hindi-language legal drama streaming television series Guilty Minds in 2022. The show’s lead cast consists of Shriya Pilgaonkar, Varun Mitra, Sugandha Garg, Kulbhushan Kharbanda, and Satish Kaushik.
  • In 2022, he was cast in the recurring role of Ashutosh Kumar/AK in the Hindi-language drama web series Crash Course.
  • Pranay Pachauri appeared as Rameez in the Hindi-language drama film The Kerala Story in 2023. Starring Adah Sharma, Yogita Bihani, Sonia Balani, and Siddhi Idnani, the film was panned by critics and Shubhra Gupta of The Indian Express described it as a “poorly-made, poorly-acted rant” that flattened Muslims into absolute evils. Despite the criticism, the film became the 3rd-highest-grossing Hindi film of 2023.
