Chris Young Biography

Tennessee‎ native Chris Young is an American country music singer and songwriter. In 2006, he was declared the winner of (Season 4) the television program Nashville Star, singing competition aired on the USA Network.

After winning Chris Young was signed to RCA Records Nashville, he then released his self-titled debut album that same year. He has produced two singles on Hot Country Songs with “Drinkin’ Me Lonely” and “You’re Gonna Love Me”.

His second album, The Man I Want to Be, was released on September 1, 2009. Her singles include the “Voices”, “Gettin’ You Home (The Black Dress Song)”, and the title track, all of which went to number 1.

Chris Young third album, known as Neon, produced two number ones in “Tomorrow” and “You” in 2011 as well as the top 20 hits “I Can Take It from There” in 2012.

His follow-up albums of the 2013’s A.M., produced three new singles with the top 5 hits “Aw Naw”, “Who I Am with You”, and “Lonely Eyes”. He has a fifth album called I’m Comin’ Over, which was released on November 13, 2015. Young gained one of the crowning achievements in country music in 2017, becoming one of the members of the Grand Ole Opry.

Chris Young Career

Chris Young started his singing career when he was in High School. He performed at different concerts in his lifetime. In 2006 he performed at a show and won. After winning he earned a contract with RCA Record in Nashville, where he released his debut song “Drinkin’ Me Lonely”.

The song peaked at number 42 on the US Billboard Hot Country Songs chart and served as the first single from his self-titled debut album, which was produced by Buddy Cannon. His second single album, “You’re Gonna Love Me”, peaked number 48.

He then released the third single “Voices”. At the CMA Music Festival in June, he promoted the single by e-mailing it to visitors at his Music Fest booth that is in May 2008. He then released his second album on Voices EP and The Man I Want to Be which was released in September 2009.

Which was produced by James Stroud and cover versions of Waylon Jennings’ and “Rose in Paradise” (as a duet with Willie Nelson) and Tony Joe White’s “Rainy Night in Georgia”.

He had another album of Young’s fourth chart; single of “Gettin’ You Home (The Black Dress Song)”, this became his first number 1 single album’s title track. He was nominated for the 2010 Academy of Country Music which is the top new vocalist, alongside Luke Bryan and Jamey Johnson.

He presented the 44th Annual Country Music Awards on November 10, 2010. He was awarded the Grammy Award for being the Best Male Country Vocal Performance for “Gettin’ You Home. He released another album of the single ”Tomorrow in February 2011.

The album neon was released in July 2011 which became his fourth straight number 1. In 2013 he served as an Ambassador for the CMA Songwriter’s Series showcased in Belfast, Dublin, London, and Paris in an attempt to showcase country music overseas.

He again released a single album of ”AW Naw” to the country radio on May 13, 2013. In November 2013 it peaked at number 3 on the Country Airplay chart. On September 17, 2013, the album, A.M., was released. The other second single album “Who I Am with You”, was released on January 20, 2014.

In August 2014 he peaked number 2 on the Country Airplay chart. On August 25, 2014, he released his third single album, known as “Lonely Eyes” which was released on country radio. He has an album that reached number 2 on the Country Airplay chart in April 2015.

He again released a new title album of ”I’m Comin’ Over which was released on May 12, 2015. It was the one which leads from the fifth studio album of the same name, which was released on November 13, 2015.

In the same month, “I’m Comin’ Over” became the sixth Number One single on the Country charts and his first since “You” in February 2012. Chris Young played the 14-show I’m Comin’ Over Tour in 2016 in support of the album.

On January 4, 2016, the album’s second single, “Think of You” with Cassadee Pope, was released. It reached number one on the Country charts in May 2016. On June 6, 2016, the third single, “Sober Saturday Night” a duet with Vince Gill was released.

In March 2017 it hit number one on the Country Airplay. He has released a country Christmas album, called It Must Be Christmas, in October 2016. On May 12, 2017, his upcoming album’s first single, “Losing Sleep” was released to country radio.

He announced the album was also titled Losing Sleep on August 23, 2017. On February 10, 2018, it reached number one on the Country Airplay. On August 29, 2017, he was invited by country legend Vince Gill to become a member of the Grand Ole Opry. He was officially inducted by Brad Paisley.
