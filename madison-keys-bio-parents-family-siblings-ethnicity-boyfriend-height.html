Career

Madison Keys first fell in love with tennis at the age of four while she watched The Championships, Wimbledon, the oldest tennis tournament in the world. She began to aspire to be like Venus Williams and politely asked her parents for her own set of white tennis dress.

Her first step to perfecting her skills was her habit of playing randomly amongst her peers, however, she joined the Quad-City Tennis Club at seven where she further groomed her skills and started competing when she was nine. As she grew older, she moved to Florida with her mom and siblings and picked up training at Evert Tennis Academy at Ten.

Madison Keys made an impressive entrance at the Ponte Vedra Beach Championship, losing only a match in 2009. The following year, the young star kept up with her outstanding skills and it certified her for the United States Open Tennis Championship which also earned her the ITF Women’s Circuit. She hit the records of being one of the youngest seven to win the Women Tennis Association Tour-Level match after she beat Alla Kudryavtseva to the title.

The ‘Pro La tennis star’ beat Serena Williams; the legendary tennis player who’s ranked No.2 in the women’s game in five games and was awarded a wild card for the Miami open. She kept her heads up high and further defeated Jill Craybas in the Grand Slam Tournament, earning her the feat of the youngest to ever achieve it, just at 16.

Keys became the first runner-up for the Charleston Cup where she lost to Kerber Angelique, she continued in her pace and was always within the range of first fifty through her game in the year 2014, however, ended the year ranking as No. 31 in the world.

Apart from defeating Williams, she also dealt mercilessly with some of the renowned professional female tennis players in the game and was ranked as No. 16 in the world after she was knocked out in the 4th round of the US Open on May 18, 2015.  In 2016, Madison Keys had a career lift; she was ranked No. 7 in the world. She got her share of pain in the game after she suffered a wrist injury, this delayed a few of her games, nevertheless, she has continued to make a name.

Apart from tennis, the young star loves baking, watching football and basketball games and also has found solace in music and reading. She has dedicated her time as an ambassador to the organization named Fearlessly Girl.
