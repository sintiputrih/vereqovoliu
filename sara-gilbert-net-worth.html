What is Sara Gilbert’s Net Worth and Salary?

Sara Gilbert is an American actress, writer, director, and TV producer with a net worth of $30 million. She gained fame for her role as Darlene Conner in the ABC series “Roseanne.”

Gilbert created and co-hosted the CBS daytime talk show “The Talk” from 2010 to 2019. She has appeared in numerous films and TV shows, including “Poison Ivy,” “ER,” and “The Big Bang Theory.”

Gilbert has also worked as a writer and director, with credits including the television film “Even the Losers” and the short film “Persona Non Grata.” She has also served as an executive producer on several projects, including the “Roseanne” reboot and “The Conners.”

Early Life and Education

Sara Gilbert was born Sara Rebecca Abeles on January 29, 1975, in Santa Monica, California. Her parents, Barbara and Harold, are both Jewish. Sara has four half-siblings. In 1984, Sara began using the last name Gilbert. She decided she wanted to become an actress at age 6. Sara attended Yale University and graduated with honors in 1997, earning a B.A. degree in art with a concentration in photography.

The Career of Sara Gilbert

Sara Gilbert made her television debut in the 1984 TV movie “Calamity Jane.” She appeared in the “Runaway Ralph” episode of “ABC Weekend Special” in 1988. Gilbert landed the role of Darlene Conner on “Roseanne” in the same year. She co-starred with Drew Barrymore in 1992’s “Poison Ivy” and won a Young Artist Award for the 1990 television film “Sudie and Simpson.”

Gilbert appeared in the 1994 film “Dead Beat” and hosted “Saturday Night Live” in 1994. She lent her voice to a 1992 episode of “The Simpsons.” She appeared in the 1999 films “Desert Blue” and “Light It Up.” In 2000, she was cast as Amy Manning on the CBS sitcom “Welcome to New York.” Gilbert also co-starred with John Cusack and Jack Black in “High Fidelity” and appeared in the Craig Ferguson film “The Big Tease.”

Gilbert reunited with Drew Barrymore for 2001’s “Riding in Cars with Boys.” She played Paula Schaeffer on five episodes of the Fox series “24” in 2002. Gilbert guest-starred on “Will & Grace” in 2003, “Strong Medicine” in 2004, and “The Clinic” in 2005. From 2004 to 2007, she played Dr. Jane Figler on 15 episodes of the NBC medical drama “ER.” She had a lead role on The WB series “Twins” from 2005 to 2006 and appeared as Fern Velch on six episodes of the CBS sitcom “The Class” from 2006 to 2007.

In 2007, Sara began playing physicist Leslie Winkle on the CBS series “The Big Bang Theory” and went on to appear in nine episodes. In 2010, she created “The Talk,” which has aired more than 2,200 episodes as of this writing.

While co-hosting “The Talk,” Sara played Irene Dudek on the 2014 CBS comedy “Bad Teacher.” She guest-starred on “The Millers” in 2013, “Web Therapy” in 2013, “Supergirl” in 2016, and “Jane the Virgin” in 2018. Gilbert appeared in six episodes of the CBS series “Living Biblically” in 2018 and starred on “Roseanne” and “The Conners.”

Gilbert left “The Talk” in August 2019. When she announced her departure a few months prior, she stated, “I think my life was slightly out of balance and I wasn’t able to spend as much time with my three kids as I’d like, and time for myself.
