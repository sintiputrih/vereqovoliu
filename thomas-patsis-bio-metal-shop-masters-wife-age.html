Netflix’s Metal Shop Masters introduced the seven best welders in the United States and put them against each other for the competition in the show. Thomas Patsis was announced the winner of the show in the season.

Since all attention is on him, this article will assist readers to learn more about his wife, children, and his career with this, Meet Thomas Patsis — Metal Shop Masters Winner.

Thomas Patsis On Metal Shop Masters

Metal Shop Masters is Netflix’s show that stirred the interest of fans who love building stuff. In the show, Jo Kay was the host, and the judges were Stephanie Hoffman and David Madero both of whom are well-known fabricators that have public works of art in various locations.

Thomas Patsis, one of the contestants of the show, won the competition and took home, $50,000 as a grand prize. He had joined the show with the seven best welders in the states. However, fans of the show on Twitter felt the other way.

They thought Leah Aripotch on the show was robbed of the title. Another user aggressively argued that both the host and the judges from the show had to be removed if they planned to return for the new edition.

About Thomas Patsis Family, Hometown

Thomas Michael Patsis was born to his father Mike Patsis and mother Ann Elizabeth Patsis in Ellsworth, Maine, United States.

Hailing from Thesprotikó, Preveza, Greece, his father attended Tantasqua Regional High School. Later on, he attended Quinsigamond Community College followed by Assumption College where he studied Biology/Chemistry. Mike worked at Town House of Pizza.

Speaking on his mother, Ann belonged to Monson, Massachusetts. She completed her high school education at Monson High School and owned Glimpses of New England Past. Later in life, she became a stay-at-home parent. She was also equally active in church and school activities.

Ann was the head coach of the varsity cross country team and assistant coach of the track & field team while her children were participating in running at Tantasqua Regional High School.

Unfortunately, Ann died on 5 August 2019 after a long battle with dementia.

Thomas’s parents stayed married until his mother’s death. Including Thomas, Ann was survived by her children eight children; (David (Deb Ledoux) Bliss, Daniel (Donna) Bliss, Michael (Ann) Bliss, Pamela (Kenneth) Hagen, Cheryl (Nick) Kachadoorian, Sandra Bliss-Coyne, Deborah (Kenneth Johnson) Elliott.

Ann was previously married to David Bliss, who after their divorce married Pearle Bliss. She was grandmother to 21 grandchildren and great-grandmother to 34 great-grandchildren and two great-great-grandchildren.
