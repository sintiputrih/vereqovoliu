Career

Gord is famous for his recurring role in the television series Orphan Black as Det. Marty Duko. He was a Canadian Screen Award nominee for Best Performance in a Guest Role in a Dramatic Series at the 5th Canadian Screen Awards in the year 2017.

In the year 2017, Gord made Pond Life into a feature film. Moreover, he has been a two-time ACTRA Award nominee, receiving nods in 2017 for Orphan Black.

And, he was also in 2019 for the film Man Running, and was a Dora Mavor Moore Award winner in 2006 for his performance in the Royal Alexandra Theatre’s production of Michael Healey’s The Innocent Eye Test.

Similarly, his other credits have included the television series Everest ’82, Durham County, Cardinal, Combat Hospital, Pure, and Chapelwaite.

And, his other films include The Definites, Maps to the Stars, and An Audience of Chairs. Moreover, he appeared in Season 3 of Reign in No Way Out.

Likewise, on stage in productions of William Shakespeare’s Hamlet for Necessary Angel Theatre, Oedipus Rex for the Stratford Festival, Maria Milisavljevic’s Abyss for Tarragon Theatre, and Christopher Morris’s The Runner for Theatre Passe Muraille.

Further, in the year 2021, this actor was announced as joining the cast of the television series Transplant in its second season.

Moreover, as a writer, his plays have included Orgy in the Lighthouse and The Trouble with Mr. Adams. Gord’s plays tend to wildly divide audiences and critics.
